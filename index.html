Biography

With an infectious passion for technology, I have more than ten years of experience in streamlining operations and improving organizational efficiency.

I have spent my entire adult life creating systems. I enjoy discovering process improvements and consider well organized structures a thing of pure beauty. "Work smart" is not just my professional philosophy, this mantra embodies my entire approach to life--period. As such, I've done just that, spending the past three years studying front-end development. I am very comfortable with technical concepts of varying complexities. Whatever I don't know, I learn, and have made a habit of doing so as quickly as possible

Most recently, I spent the past year at Coalition Technologies, a digital marketing firm in Culver City, California. While there, I learned the ins and outs of SEO, conversion optimization, campaign management, and keyword research to name a few. While 'learning on the job', I managed campaigns for ten of the agency's largest accounts.

Prior to Coalition, I was an account manager at Powered Office, a California-based internet startup aimed at empowering small to mid size businesses via website and webapp creation etc., where I managed a portfolio of forty accounts. Before working on the technical side of things, I managed their Customer Service department where I implemented new systems and tools via Google Apps, etc.

To date, via embracing both the creative and quantitative and establishing an authentic rapport with people of diverse backgrounds and standings. Countless projects and numerous ups-and-downs later, I'm still as obsessed as ever with efficacy and efficiency in employee production and agency acheivement.

I currently reside in sunny Los Angeles, California and continue to work in tech, both professionally and as a hobby. I have a keen appreciation for good design, which plays a major role in every facet of my life, bordering on obsessive. I also play guitar as a part of a local band, and enjoy physical activities and learning new languages when time allows, both of which are incredibly rewwarding.
